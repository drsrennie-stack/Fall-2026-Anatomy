Your first week, made simple

Don't try to read everything today. Do the six things on this page in your first week, and you're set. The full syllabus is your reference for the rest of the term.

This is a flipped course: you meet each concept first on your own, then class time is spent using it. That's the whole idea, here it is in three beats.

Week 1 checklist

0 of 6 done

1

Set up your section in the Mastery OS 5 min

Open the Mastery OS and pick your class, Mon/Wed Afternoon, Tue/Thu Early, or Tue/Thu Evening. Every date (your exams, TBLs, and nightly pre-work) then matches your section.

Open Mastery OS →
2

Log into Canvas and turn on notifications 5 min

Canvas is where announcements, assignments, and InteDashboard live. Log in with your Solano account and switch on announcement notifications. Check it at least three times a week.

3

Do your first pre-work the night before class 30 min

The evening before each class, watch the assigned concept lecture and finish the guided notes. Click any day on the calendar to see exactly what's due the night before.

Open the calendar →
4

Come to every class this first week

Attendance in week 1 is required. If you miss a class this week your seat may be given to a student on the waitlist. This is also where we form teams and set up InteDashboard together.

5

Read & sign the Lab Device Policy before lab

We work with human donors. No phones, watches, or cameras in the lab, ever. You must read and sign the policy before your first lab session. Students under 18 need a guardian signature too.

Read the policy →
6

Meet your team on day one

You'll be placed on a team for the term. Before you leave the first class, exchange contact info, pick one group chat, and agree on a 24-hour reply standard.

!

Need accommodations? Set it up in week 1.

Contact Solano's Accessibility Services Center (ASC, Building 400 Room 407, 707-864-7136, ASC@solano.edu) as early as possible, then tell Dr. Rennie your approved accommodations through the Canvas Inbox during the first week.

One habit makes this course work

Do the pre-work the night before. If you walk in having watched the lecture, the checks are easy and class actually makes sense. Everything else is just showing up and doing the reps.
